<h1>📦 Samsung Supply Chain & Logistics Analytics Dashboard</h1>

<h2>📊 Project Overview</h2>
<p>
This project presents an <b>end-to-end Supply Chain & Logistics Analytics Dashboard</b> built using <b>Power BI</b>.
The dashboard analyzes key operational metrics across customers, suppliers, inventory, and shipment performance 
to help businesses monitor efficiency, identify bottlenecks, and support data-driven decision making.
</p>

<p>
The analysis focuses on revenue generation, supplier performance, shipment delays, inventory health,
and product demand trends.
</p>

<hr>

<h2>🛠 Tools & Technologies Used</h2>
<ul>
<li><b>Power BI</b> – Data visualization and dashboard development</li>
<li><b>Excel / CSV Dataset</b> – Data source</li>
<li><b>DAX (Data Analysis Expressions)</b> – Measures and calculated fields</li>
<li><b>Power Query</b> – Data cleaning and transformation</li>
</ul>

<hr>

<h2>📁 Dashboard Modules</h2>
<p>The dashboard is divided into multiple analytical sections:</p>

<h3>1️⃣ Customer Analytics</h3>
<p>Analyzes revenue contribution from customers and product categories.</p>

<h4>Key KPIs</h4>
<ul>
<li>Gross Revenue: <b>186.86M</b></li>
<li>Total Revenue: <b>176.95M</b></li>
<li>Total Profit: <b>48.56M</b></li>
<li>Profit Margin: <b>27.44%</b></li>
<li>Perfect Order Rate: <b>75%</b></li>
<li>Discount Amount: <b>9.92M</b></li>
</ul>

<h4>Key Insights</h4>
<ul>
<li>Amazon and Flipkart generate the highest revenue.</li>
<li>Smartphones contribute the largest share of product revenue.</li>
<li>Online sales channels account for the largest portion of total revenue.</li>
<li>Profit margin across channels remains relatively balanced.</li>
</ul>

<hr>

<h3>2️⃣ Shipment Performance Analysis</h3>

<h4>Key KPIs</h4>
<ul>
<li>Total Shipments: <b>8K</b></li>
<li>Shipping Cost: <b>19.42M</b></li>
<li>Total Delays: <b>573</b></li>
<li>Delivered Shipments: <b>6K</b></li>
<li>Shipment Quantity: <b>3M</b></li>
<li>Delivery Rate: <b>75%</b></li>
</ul>

<h4>Key Insights</h4>
<ul>
<li>Maersk Line experienced the highest shipment delays.</li>
<li>Major causes of delays include:</li>
<ul>
<li>Carrier issues</li>
<li>Documentation delays</li>
<li>Port congestion</li>
<li>Customs clearance</li>
</ul>
<li>Shipping cost rises steadily toward the end of the year, peaking in December.</li>
</ul>

<hr>

<h3>3️⃣ Inventory & Production Analysis</h3>

<h4>Key KPIs</h4>
<ul>
<li>Inventory Value: <b>160K</b></li>
<li>Safety Stock: <b>89K</b></li>
<li>Inventory Turnover Rate: <b>1.17</b></li>
<li>Days of Inventory: <b>0.31K</b></li>
<li>Defective Units: <b>54</b></li>
</ul>

<h4>Key Insights</h4>
<ul>
<li>Inventory value fluctuates throughout the year with peaks during Q4.</li>
<li>Products such as <b>Galaxy S24 Ultra</b> and <b>Galaxy Buds</b> show higher defect rates.</li>
<li>Inventory monitoring helps balance safety stock and reorder points.</li>
</ul>

<hr>

<h3>4️⃣ Supplier Performance Analysis</h3>

<h4>Key KPIs</h4>
<ul>
<li>Total Unit Cost: <b>78.13M</b></li>
<li>Order Quantity: <b>129K</b></li>
<li>Average Lead Time: <b>11.53 days</b></li>
<li>Average Quality Score: <b>96.63</b></li>
</ul>

<h4>Key Insights</h4>
<ul>
<li>Suppliers from China, Japan, South Korea, and Taiwan have higher lead times.</li>
<li>BOE Technology, Samsung Vietnam, and Taiwan Semiconductor contribute the highest order quantities.</li>
<li>Taiwan Semiconductor and Sony Semiconductor have the highest quality scores.</li>
</ul>

<hr>

<h2>📈 Key Business Insights</h2>
<ul>
<li>E-commerce platforms drive the majority of revenue.</li>
<li>Shipment delays are mainly caused by logistics and documentation issues.</li>
<li>Inventory spikes occur in the last quarter indicating seasonal demand.</li>
<li>Supplier performance varies based on lead time and quality.</li>
</ul>
